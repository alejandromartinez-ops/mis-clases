🦁 Reported Speech 🐆

A day at the Wildlife Park: Statements & Commands

💬 1. Reported Speech: Statements

We use reported speech to talk about the things somebody said in the past. To do this, we usually change the tenses (we move them one step back into the past).

Subject + said (that) + Subject + changed verb + ...
Direct Speech (What they say) Reported Speech (What we report)
"I'm going to the wildlife park." He said he was going to the wildlife park.
"I saw a leopard." She said she had seen a leopard.
"I've read a book about penguins." He said he had read a book about penguins.
"I will take more photos next time." She said she would take more photos next time.

🗣️ 2. The difference: Said vs. Told

When we report things, we use said for general speech and told for commands (orders) or instructions.

Using "Said"

We use it for general statements. We do NOT need an object after said. We DO change the tense.

Subject + said (that) + Subject + Verb...

"They don't like noisy visitors!"

➔ She said they didn't like noisy visitors.

Using "Told" (Commands)

We use it to give instructions. We NEED an object (me, us, him) after told. We do NOT change the tense, we use the infinitive.

Subject + told + Object + (not) to + base verb

"Be very quiet."

➔ The tour guide told us to be very quiet.

"Don't take photos."

➔ The teacher told us not to take photos.
